Top 10 Online Training Software to Create & Sell Online Courses

With everyone shifting to the online environment since the onset of the novel coronavirus pandemic, it’s not surprising that there’s a high demand for online courses.

Whether we’re talking about corporate education programs or K12 and university classes, online courses are now essential. You can easily create and sell your own if you have the right eLearning authoring tools.

So, let’s check out some of the best tools that can not only enhance your online teaching efforts, but also help others improve their teaching and learning by using the courses you’ve created.

1. ProProfs Training Maker

With the global e-learning market expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.85% by 2025, there’s no shortage of online training software, but it’s crucial to find the one that suits your needs.

ProProfs Training Maker is among the most comprehensive solutions that allow users to build online courses, whether for corporate or educational purposes.

This e-learning authoring tool has an extensive library of hundreds of pre-made courses that you can use right away.

You can just as easily start from scratch and upload any file type, choose your themes, and fully customize almost every aspect of your course.

Develop educational tests and quizzes, open up discussions, make use of intricate reports and analytics, and quickly sell your courses through either ProProfs Gateway or Stripe.

It’s user-friendly with a minimal learning curve, and affordable to suit anyone’s budget.

2. Teachable

While not as feature-packed, Teachable can be an excellent online training software if you’re an instructor who needs additional passive income.

It’s straightforward to master, making it an excellent choice for those new to e-learning. Everything can be accessed from the main dashboard so that you can create a full-blown online course in a matter of hours.

Since the main focus of Teachable is to help you sell courses, most of their features are designed to make this process easier.

This online training software gives you access to its affiliate marketing tools that help you get the word out. You can sell your courses to everyone across the world since over 130 international currencies are accepted.

3. Ruzuku

Ruzuku is an easy-to-use online training software that can be suitable for companies looking to make additional cash by selling online courses.

Instructors can use learner dashboards to gain insight into their performance, and you can decide whether your learners get access to all the materials at once or just one at a time.

The only things lacking are the customization features. While you do have some basic customization options, other e-learning authoring tools offer more choices.

Marketing and selling your Ruzuku courses is made easy through its affiliates.

4. WizIQ

WizIQ is a cloud-based online training software that doesn’t require any downloads or apps, and it can be accessed from any device.

You can use it to develop a full-blown virtual classroom, make use of its HD audio and video for real-time classes, or use the recording tools for asynchronous learning and teaching.

All your courses can be sold to learners from all across the world.

5. LearnWorlds

Another cloud-based solution for creating and selling your courses is LearnWorlds. It can be used in school environments, for corporate training, or even for customer education.

The platform enables users to use multimedia content in your courses, add engaging videos, and even create interactive eBooks. You can even use it as a page builder and a blogging tool.

It has basic gamification features such as badges to keep the learners engaged and increase the appeal (and the price value) of your course.

6. SkillShare

If you’ve been looking to sell your online courses, you’ve likely come across SkillShare, one of the most heavily marketed online training software.

It enables users to create a wide variety of different courses that cover topics from lifestyle and meditation to business strategy and web design.

It’s not for everyone, however. Most of the learners on SkillShare approach learning in a bit more casual way, so it’s not ideal for serious businesses and traditional educational institutions.

7. LearnDash

LearnDash is jam-packed with features that allow users to create detailed courses and extensive eLearning projects.

It’s a plugin for WordPress sites, so you can create self-hosted courses and have full control over how and when you’ll sell them.

While it’s a comprehensive solution, it features quite a learning curve and can be challenging to master.

8. Thinkific

Thinkific is one of the favorites among online training software users because it’s easy to get accustomed to, and allows you to create, update, and change your courses with ease.

Even if you have no previous experience with eLearning authoring tools or course creation, you’ll likely find Thinkific simple to use.

Selling your courses online is a breeze, and you can use affiliate programs to enhance your marketing, as well as offer coupons and discounts to attract more customers.

9. TalentLMS

You can use the materials you already have and upload them to TalentLMS, or create and launch brand new online courses without a hitch.

The software boasts all the necessary features that enhance your course creation process, and it can be integrated with various video conferencing tools as needed if you want to host live lessons.

You can use Stripe or PayPal to sell your courses to global audiences.

10. iSpring Learn

Finally, iSpring Learn is designed to help create employee training courses but can be useful in educational institutions as well.

Its greatest benefit is that it offers unlimited storage, which means that you can keep all of your courses available and continue selling and reselling them all without deleting old courses after you create new ones.

It’s easy to use and get accustomed to and will provide you with all the tools you need to create and sell your own online courses.

The Bottom Line

Finding the right online training software to create and sell your own courses can be complicated as there are so many solutions available on the market.

Most of the eLearning authoring tools mentioned here offer a free trial, so you should take advantage of it. Test different solutions, play around with them a little, and find the one that suits you best.
